# Game Concept

## Working Title
Echo Escape

## Short Pitch
Echo Escape is a small 2D puzzle-platform game where the player records a short sequence of their own actions and then uses a replaying copy of themselves to solve the level. The vertical slice will focus on one polished stage with timing, risk, and clear feedback.

## Target Player Experience
The player should feel clever for planning around their own recorded movement. The game should be readable, restartable, and tense enough that treasure feels valuable without making failure feel unfair.

## Core Mechanic
The player moves through the level, presses a record key, performs a short action sequence, and then replays that sequence through a copy or echo. The echo can hold switches, trigger pressure plates, distract hazards, or help with timing-based traversal.

Treasure chests spawn randomly and can award rare loot. If the player dies later in that section, the newly gained loot is lost.

## Planned Vertical Slice
- One playable level
- Player movement and collision
- Record and replay system for one echo
- Two or three simple level interactions such as doors, switches, or moving hazards
- Random chest spawn with item reward logic
- UI for health, carried loot, and restart feedback
- Win and lose states
- Basic sound and visual feedback
- Short testing log showing improvements

## Scope Control
The project will avoid multiplayer, large open worlds, complicated enemy AI, and many levels. The goal is a small but complete and stable vertical slice built around one memorable mechanic.

## Legal, Ethical, Social, Accessibility, and Security Notes
- Use original assets, Unity built-in assets, or clearly credited free assets.
- Avoid collecting personal data.
- Use readable fonts, clear contrast, and simple controls.
- Include a restart option, clear player feedback, and consistent indicators for risk and loot loss.
